About Me

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Cancer Sciences at Cancer Research UK (CRUK) Scotland Institute and the University of Glasgow, under the guidance of Prof. Crispin Miller and Prof. Kevin Blyth. My research focuses on understanding cell interactions within the tumor microenvironment using computational and machine learning methods to gain insights into cancer biology, specifically by developing methods to combine spatial transcriptomics and histopathology image modalities using deep learning. I am also part of the PREDICT-Meso project, a European consortium dedicated to exploring the early stages of mesothelioma development to design effective diagnostic and treatment strategies.

Previously, I completed a Master’s by Research at IIIT Hyderabad, where I worked with the Cancer Diagnostics (DIAG) lab under Prof. Vinod P.K. and Prof. C.V. Jawahar. My research focused on deep learning-based automated classification of tumors using histopathology images and collecting large datasets from partner institutions.

Earlier, as a Research Fellow at iHub Data, Hyderabad, I developed a low-parameter model for diagnosing cardiovascular diseases in resource-constrained environments, reducing input requirements to enhance its usability on edge devices. During my undergraduate studies at Maharaja Agrasen Institute of Technology, Delhi, I worked on evolutionary algorithms, COVID-19 prediction models, and environmental monitoring using machine learning during an internship at IIT-BHU.

I am passionate about addressing critical challenges in medical sciences through artificial intelligence, with a focus on cancer and other life-threatening diseases.
